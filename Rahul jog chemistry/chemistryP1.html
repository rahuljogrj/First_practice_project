Lesson objectives

In this lesson, you will:

  • describe the technique of chromatography
  • explain that a chromatogram provides information about the composition of a mixture
  • calculate Rf values
  • use Rf values to identify the components of a mixture.

Key definitions

  • chromatogram: the absorbent paper from paper chromatography showing the separation of different coloured substances
  • paper chromatography: a process to separate a mixture of coloured substances using absorbent paper
  • retardation factor (Rf): this is sometimes called the retention factor; it is calculated as the distance moved by a spot of dye (from the pencil line) divided by the distance moved by the solvent front (from the pencil line) on a chromatogram.

Miconceptions

  • Chromatography can provide the potential for confusion and so the whole process should be demonstrated with the reason for drawing the origin line in pencil explained. Likewise, a detailed demonstration of how to measure the solvent front and the distance travelled by a spot should be given.
  • It is crucial that students realise that each separation technique used is dictated by the type of the substances being separated. This should be linked to the properties of the substances, solubility, boiling point etc.
